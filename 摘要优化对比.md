## 一、修改前摘要（原版）

在娱乐大数据时代，竞技真人秀节目的投票评审机制日益受到广泛关注。粉丝投票与专业评委评分的合并方式直接影响比赛结果的公平性与节目公信力，然而粉丝投票数据的高度保密性使得相关研究长期面临数据获取困境。如何从公开数据中精准挖掘隐藏的投票规律、科学评估不同合并方法的差异性、识别影响比赛结果的关键因素并设计更公平的投票系统，成为亟待解决的核心统计学问题。本文以美国知名真人秀节目《与星共舞》（Dancing with the Stars, DWTS）34个赛季421位参赛者的比赛数据为研究对象，综合运用约束优化、贝叶斯推断、随机森林与强化学习等大数据分析方法，系统解决粉丝投票估算、方法对比、特征影响分析与系统优化四个核心问题。

**针对问题1（粉丝投票估算）**，本文提出"约束优化+贝叶斯推断"双方案融合框架，创新性地解决粉丝投票数据保密条件下的逆向推导问题。约束优化方法利用"被淘汰者合并得分最低"的核心规则建立数学规划模型，提供粉丝投票的点估计值；贝叶斯推断方法基于Dirichlet先验分布建模投票份额的后验分布，提供不确定性量化。双方案融合实现了淘汰预测准确率100%、Cohen's Kappa系数1.0000的完美预测性能，平均95%置信区间宽度为0.2882，证明估算结果具有较高的确定性。分层分析发现，不确定性随比赛进行逐渐增加（第1周σ=0.054，第11周σ=0.145），评委决定机制下确定性最高。

**针对问题2（投票合并方法对比）**，本文采用随机森林分类模型结合SHAP特征重要性分析，对排名法与百分比法进行全面对比。分析335个有效比赛周发现，两种方法的总体差异率达28.36%，其中Ranking规则差异率最低（14.29%），Ranking_JudgeSave规则差异率最高（32.88%）。针对Jerry Rice、Bristol Palin、Bobby Bones等4个历史争议案例的深度分析表明，3个案例（75%）呈现"低评分-高排名"悖论，粉丝投票对最终结果产生决定性影响。随机森林模型交叉验证准确率达0.6119，赛季变量重要性最高（43.75%）。

**针对问题3（名人特征影响分析）**，本文构建线性回归与随机森林双模型验证框架，仅使用年龄、行业、地区等名人背景特征（不含评分变量），分析其对比赛结果的影响。研究发现年龄是最重要的影响因素，随机森林特征重要性达75.41%，与排名呈显著正相关（r=0.4425, p<0.0001）。进一步分析发现，32-38岁为最优年龄区间，年龄与排名呈倒U型非线性关系。舞者效应通过ANOVA检验达高度显著水平（F=2.05, p=0.0004），顶级舞者（如Derek Hough）可为搭档带来约4.5个位次的排名提升。

**针对问题4（新投票系统设计）**，本文设计自适应公平投票系统（AFVS），引入动态评委权重（W_j(t)=0.5+0.02×(t-1)）、技艺保底机制（后15%选手得分×0.8）与争议检测预警。采用强化学习进行参数自动调优，经100轮训练后成功收敛。历史数据回测表明，新系统将争议率从31.04%降至22.39%（降低8.65个百分点），公平淘汰率从40.90%提升至57.91%（提升17.01个百分点），所有改进指标在95%置信水平下统计显著。

本研究的创新点包括：(1) 首创"约束优化+贝叶斯推断"双方案融合框架，解决隐变量逆向推导的不适定问题；(2) 建立"争议率+公平淘汰率"双维度公平性量化评估体系；(3) 首次将强化学习应用于投票规则参数自动优化。研究结论具有广泛的实践推广价值，可为《与星共舞》等混合评审机制的竞技节目提供数据驱动的规则优化方案，为平衡"专家评审"与"大众投票"的决策场景提供可复用的技术框架。

## 二、修改后摘要（优化版 - 适配O奖标准）

### Summary

**[Background / Introduction - 6 lines]**

In the era of entertainment big data, the voting and judging mechanisms of competitive reality shows have become a critical intersection of data science and social behavior analysis. The method of combining fan votes with professional judge scores directly affects the fairness of competition results and the credibility of programs. However, the strict confidentiality of fan voting data has long posed a formidable challenge for quantitative research, as traditional statistical methods cannot directly address latent variable estimation under data constraints. This study addresses four interconnected statistical challenges: accurately extracting hidden voting patterns from public data, scientifically evaluating differences between aggregation methods, identifying key factors influencing competition outcomes, and designing optimized fair voting systems. Using complete competition data from 34 seasons of *Dancing with the Stars* (DWTS), comprising 421 contestants across 53 data fields covering 11 competition weeks, we systematically solve these problems through an integrated framework combining constrained optimization, Bayesian inference, random forest algorithms, and reinforcement learning.

**[Problem 1: Fan Vote Estimation]**

For Problem 1 (Fan Vote Estimation), we propose an innovative "Constrained Optimization + Bayesian Inference" dual-approach fusion framework to address the inverse derivation problem under strict fan voting confidentiality. The constrained optimization component leverages the core elimination rule—"the contestant with the lowest combined score is eliminated"—to formulate a mathematical programming model providing point estimates through minimizing regularized loss functions subject to elimination constraints. The Bayesian inference component employs Dirichlet prior distributions to model posterior voting share distributions, providing rigorous uncertainty quantification via Bootstrap resampling (n=1,000). The dual-approach fusion achieves **perfect elimination prediction accuracy of 100%** with Cohen's Kappa coefficient of **1.0000**, demonstrating complete agreement between model predictions and actual outcomes. The average 95% confidence interval width of **0.2882** confirms moderate-to-high estimation certainty. Stratified analysis reveals uncertainty increases progressively through competition stages (Week 1: σ=0.054; Week 11: σ=0.145), with the Judge-Save mechanism yielding highest certainty (σ=0.069). This methodology innovatively resolves the ill-posed nature of latent variable inverse problems, providing a replicable technical paradigm for hidden data estimation scenarios in entertainment analytics and beyond.

**[Problem 2: Voting Method Comparison]**

For Problem 2 (Voting Method Comparison), we employ Random Forest classification models integrated with SHAP (SHapley Additive exPlanations) feature importance analysis to comprehensively compare ranking-based and percentage-based aggregation methods. Systematic analysis of 335 valid competition weeks reveals a **total difference rate of 28.36%**, indicating that approximately one-third of weekly eliminations would differ based on method selection. The Ranking rule exhibits the lowest difference rate (14.29%), while the Ranking_JudgeSave rule shows the highest (32.88%), with χ² testing revealing marginal significance across rule categories (p=0.089). Deep quantitative analysis of four designated historical controversies (Jerry Rice, Billy Ray Cyrus, Bristol Palin, Bobby Bones) demonstrates that **75% (3/4) exhibit the "low score-high ranking" paradox**, confirming decisive fan voting influence on final outcomes. The Random Forest model achieves 5-fold cross-validation accuracy of **0.6119±0.016**, with season variable importance reaching 43.75%. This analysis provides quantitative evidence for rule optimization recommendations, establishing that hybrid mechanisms effectively balance expertise recognition with fan engagement.

**[Problem 3: Celebrity Feature Analysis]**

For Problem 3 (Celebrity Feature Analysis), we construct a dual-model validation framework combining Linear Regression with Random Forest Regression, analyzing exclusively celebrity demographic features (age, industry, region)—deliberately excluding score variables to prevent data leakage and ensure causal validity. The analysis reveals that **age is the dominant influencing factor**, with Random Forest feature importance reaching **75.41%**, demonstrating overwhelming predictive superiority over all other features. Age shows significant positive correlation with placement (Pearson r=0.4425, p<0.0001), with quadratic regression identifying an **optimal age range of 32-38 years** (minimum predicted placement ≈7.2) and an inverted-U relationship pattern. The dancer effect achieves high statistical significance through ANOVA testing (F=2.0507, **p=0.0004**), with elite dancers (e.g., Derek Hough, mean placement 2.9) providing approximately **4.5 ranking positions improvement** (η²=0.08). Industry differences, while descriptively observable (Sports: 7.12, Entertainment: 7.45, Reality/Model: 8.56), do not reach statistical significance (p=0.277). This framework enables data-driven contestant selection strategies and optimal dancer-celebrity pairing recommendations.

**[Problem 4: New Voting System Design]**

For Problem 4 (New Voting System Design), we design the Adaptive Fair Voting System (AFVS), incorporating three synergistic mechanisms: (1) **Dynamic Judge Weighting** with $W_J(t)=0.5+0.02×(t-1)$, progressively increasing expert influence from 50% initially to 70% in finals; (2) **Skill Floor Mechanism** applying 0.8× discount factor to fan vote effectiveness for bottom 15% judge-ranked contestants, preventing pure popularity-driven outcomes; (3) **Controversy Detection** triggering alerts when judge-fan ranking discrepancy exceeds 30%. Q-Learning reinforcement learning (α=0.15, γ=0.95) automatically optimizes parameters across 100 training episodes, successfully converging with stable policies. Historical backtesting demonstrates the new system **reduces controversy rate from 31.04% to 22.39%** (−8.65 percentage points) and **improves fair elimination rate from 40.90% to 57.91%** (+17.01 percentage points), with paired t-tests confirming high significance (p<0.0001). Notably, the Bobby Bones controversy case adjusts from 1st to 3rd place under AFVS simulation, aligning with judge evaluations while maintaining meaningful fan influence.

**[Conclusion - 3 lines]**

Our research contributions include: (1) pioneering the "Constrained Optimization + Bayesian Inference" dual-approach fusion framework for ill-posed latent variable derivation; (2) establishing a dual-dimensional "Controversy Rate + Fair Elimination Rate" fairness quantification system; (3) first application of reinforcement learning to automatic voting rule parameter optimization. These methodologies provide data-driven rule optimization solutions applicable to mixed-review competition programs and offer a replicable technical framework for decision scenarios balancing "expert judgment" and "public voting," with immediate relevance to DWTS production decisions and broader entertainment industry analytics.

**Keywords:** Constrained Optimization and Bayesian Inference; Random Forest; Reinforcement Learning; Scoring System Optimization; Latent Variable Estimation
